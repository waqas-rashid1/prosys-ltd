## Section: The problem

### Eyebrow

AI pilots are easy. Production AI is not.

### Headline

The hard part is not building an AI demo. It is making AI safe enough to run inside the business.

### Body

Companies are experimenting with copilots, agents, automations, and internal knowledge tools. But once those systems need to access sensitive data, make recommendations, trigger actions, or integrate with existing software, the risk profile changes.

Without the right architecture, AI creates new problems: data exposure, hallucinated outputs, uncontrolled actions, unclear ownership, rising model costs, weak auditability, and systems no one can confidently operate after launch.

PROSYS LTD helps teams design AI with the controls production work requires.

### Problem cards

#### AI is disconnected from real workflows

The pilot works in isolation, but it does not connect to the CRM, ERP, helpdesk, database, document store, approval process, or human team that actually owns the work.

#### AI can act without enough control

Agents need role-based permissions, action limits, approvals, logs, escalation rules, and clear boundaries before they can safely operate.

#### AI quality is hard to measure

Teams need visibility into hallucinations, retrieval accuracy, cost, latency, fallback behavior, user feedback, and failures over time.

#### AI risk is owned by no one

Security, compliance, operations, and engineering all need a shared control model for what AI can access, decide, and do.

## Section: Lifecycle

### Eyebrow

Our operating model

### Headline

Assess. Build. Govern. Integrate. Secure. Improve.

### Body

Safe AI deployment is not a single build phase. It is a lifecycle. We start by identifying the right use case, then design the workflow, build the system, connect it to existing tools, add controls, test the risks, and monitor performance after launch.

### Lifecycle steps

#### 01. Assess

We identify high-value AI opportunities, map business workflows, review data readiness, estimate ROI, and define risk boundaries.

Deliverables:

- AI opportunity map
- Workflow and data assessment
- Risk and feasibility review
- Prioritized implementation roadmap
- Success metrics

#### 02. Build

We design and implement the AI workflow, agent, knowledge system, or automation needed to solve the defined business problem.

Deliverables:

- Agent or workflow architecture
- System prompts and tool logic
- Retrieval or knowledge layer
- User experience and handoff design
- Working pilot or production release

#### 03. Govern

We add the control layer required for safe operation.

Deliverables:

- Role-based access rules
- Human approval gates
- Audit logs
- Escalation paths
- Risk scoring
- Kill switch or rollback process

#### 04. Integrate

We connect AI to the systems where work already happens.

Deliverables:

- CRM, ERP, helpdesk, database, or document integrations
- API and webhook architecture
- Authentication and permission handling
- Data flow documentation
- Operational handoff

#### 05. Secure

We test for risky behavior before the system reaches users or customers.

Deliverables:

- Prompt injection testing
- Data leakage checks
- Agent action abuse testing
- Hallucination and reliability review
- Guardrail implementation
- Security and risk report

#### 06. Improve

We monitor real-world behavior and improve the system over time.

Deliverables:

- Quality dashboard
- Cost and latency monitoring
- Failure and escalation tracking
- Usage analytics
- Feedback loops
- Monthly improvement roadmap

## Section: Why we exist

### Headline

Most AI work stops before operations. We built PROSYS LTD for the handoff after the demo.

### Body

AI tools have made prototyping easier than ever. But production is still hard. The moment AI touches customer data, internal systems, employee workflows, approvals, or regulated processes, the work changes. Teams need architecture, controls, integrations, documentation, testing, and monitoring.

PROSYS LTD exists to own that operational gap. We help companies decide where AI belongs, build it safely, connect it to real systems, and monitor it after launch.

## Section: Principles

### Principle 1: Outcomes over novelty

We do not recommend AI where a simpler system would work better. Every engagement starts with the business outcome and workflow reality.

### Principle 2: Controls before autonomy

AI should not gain access, tools, or decision rights without clear permissions, approvals, logs, and escalation paths.

### Principle 3: Integration over isolation

AI systems create value when they connect to existing tools, data, people, and processes. Standalone demos are not enough.

### Principle 4: Measurable after launch

Production AI requires visibility into quality, cost, latency, risk, and business impact.

### Principle 5: Built to be inherited

We document systems so client teams can understand, operate, and extend what we build.

## Section: Team positioning

### Headline

Built by engineers, operators, and AI implementation specialists.

### Body

Our team works across product engineering, applied AI, automation, infrastructure, security, and growth. The shared standard is production ownership: clear scope, documented decisions, working releases, and operational handoff.

## Section: Standards

### Headline

Designed for serious environments.

### Body

Our engineering practice is modeled on control objectives used in mature software organizations: least-privilege access, secure data handling, documented architecture, reviewable releases, audit-ready logs, and operational monitoring. Formal certifications should only be referenced where verified.

## Problem

AI pilots are easy. Production AI is not.

The hard part is not building an AI demo. It is making AI safe enough to run inside the business.

Companies are experimenting with copilots, agents, automations, and internal knowledge tools. But once those systems need to access sensitive data, make recommendations, trigger actions, or integrate with existing software, the risk profile changes.

Without the right architecture, AI creates new problems: data exposure, hallucinated outputs, uncontrolled actions, unclear ownership, rising model costs, weak auditability, and systems no one can confidently operate after launch.

PROSYS LTD helps teams design AI with the controls production work requires.

## Services

A focused service model for safe AI deployment.

AI Readiness and Strategy: Identify the safest, highest-ROI AI opportunities before you build.

Secure AI Agents and Workflow Automation: Build AI agents that operate inside real business workflows with permissions, approvals, and human oversight.

AI Governance and Control Systems: Define what AI can see, decide, change, and escalate.

Secure Enterprise Knowledge Systems: Give teams AI access to internal knowledge without exposing sensitive data.

AI Integration with Existing Systems: Connect AI to the tools your business already runs on.

AI Security, Monitoring, and Optimization: Test, monitor, and improve AI after launch.

## Lifecycle

Assess. Build. Govern. Integrate. Secure. Improve.

Safe AI deployment is not a single build phase. It is a lifecycle. We start by identifying the right use case, then design the workflow, build the system, connect it to existing tools, add controls, test the risks, and monitor performance after launch.

## Who this is for

For companies ready to move AI into actual work.

We are a fit when AI needs to interact with business-critical data, systems, teams, or decisions. That includes companies launching AI-enabled products, modernizing internal operations, or creating governed agent workflows across departments.

## Why PROSYS LTD

We build the parts that make AI usable in production.

Production AI requires more than prompts and APIs. It requires software engineering, data flow design, security thinking, workflow mapping, governance, monitoring, and operational ownership. PROSYS LTD brings these disciplines together under one delivery standard.
